Yellowstone National Park FAQ

Yellowstone National Park is located primarily in northwestern Wyoming, but it also extends into southern Montana and eastern Idaho. The park encompasses an impressive 2.2 million acres of diverse ecosystems.

Geographic Details:

  • Wyoming: Contains about 96% of the park (2.1 million acres)
  • Montana: Contains about 3% of the park (northern sections)
  • Idaho: Contains about 1% of the park (western edge)

The park sits atop the Yellowstone Caldera, a large volcanic caldera and supervolcano. It's situated on a high plateau with elevations ranging from 5,282 feet at the northern entrance to 11,358 feet at Eagle Peak.

Access Points: The park has five entrances - North (Gardiner, MT), Northeast (Cooke City, MT), East (Cody, WY), South (Jackson, WY), and West (West Yellowstone, MT).

Yellowstone holds the distinction of being America's first national park, established on March 1, 1872, by President Ulysses S. Grant. This groundbreaking designation made it the world's first national park, setting a precedent for conservation efforts globally.

World-Famous Features:

  • Geothermal Wonders: Home to over 10,000 hydrothermal features, including geysers, hot springs, fumaroles, and mud pots
  • Wildlife Sanctuary: One of the last intact large ecosystems in the northern temperate zone
  • Geological Marvel: Sits atop an active supervolcano with a caldera measuring 34 by 45 miles
  • Biodiversity Hub: Protects 67 mammal species, 285 bird species, and countless other wildlife

The park contains over half of the world's active geysers and the largest concentration of thermal features on Earth. Its pristine wilderness, dramatic landscapes, and successful conservation efforts have made it a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1978.

Yellowstone also played a crucial role in the establishment of the National Park Service in 1916 and continues to serve as a model for conservation worldwide.

Old Faithful is the most famous geyser in the world and Yellowstone's most iconic attraction. This remarkable natural phenomenon gets its name from its exceptional reliability and predictability, making it a favorite among millions of visitors annually.

Eruption Characteristics:

  • Frequency: Erupts approximately every 60-110 minutes (average: 90 minutes)
  • Height: Water reaches 100-185 feet into the air during eruptions
  • Duration: Each eruption lasts 1.5 to 5 minutes
  • Volume: Discharges 3,700-8,400 gallons of water per eruption
  • Temperature: Water temperature reaches approximately 204°F (95.5°C)

How It Works: Old Faithful's underground plumbing system consists of a narrow tube that extends down at least 76 feet. As superheated water rises through this constricted passage, pressure builds until it erupts in spectacular fashion.

The geyser was named by members of the Washburn-Langford-Doane Expedition in 1870, who were amazed by its consistent performance. Today, park rangers can predict eruptions within a 10-minute window based on the duration of the previous eruption.

Visitor Experience: The Old Faithful Visitor Education Center offers excellent views, and the surrounding boardwalk provides safe viewing from multiple angles. The adjacent Old Faithful Inn, built in 1904, is the world's largest log hotel.

The ideal time to visit Yellowstone depends on your interests and tolerance for crowds, but late spring through early fall generally offers the most accessible and comfortable experience.

Season-by-Season Breakdown:

🌸 Spring (April-May):

  • Wildlife is very active as animals emerge from winter
  • Fewer crowds and lower accommodation prices
  • Some roads may still be closed due to snow
  • Weather can be unpredictable with possible snow

☀️ Summer (June-August):

  • All roads and facilities are fully open
  • Warmest weather with daytime highs 70-85°F
  • Peak crowds - expect traffic and busy attractions
  • Best time for hiking and camping
  • Longest daylight hours for sightseeing

🍂 Fall (September-October):

  • Many consider this the BEST time to visit
  • Stunning fall colors and crisp, clear weather
  • Wildlife active during mating season
  • Fewer crowds than summer but all amenities still open
  • Elk bugling season (September-October)

❄️ Winter (November-March):

  • Magical snowy landscapes and fewer visitors
  • Limited access - most roads closed to regular vehicles
  • Snowmobile and snow coach tours available
  • Wildlife viewing opportunities, especially wolves
  • Extremely cold temperatures (-20°F to 20°F)

Pro Tip: September and early October offer the perfect balance of good weather, active wildlife, beautiful scenery, and manageable crowds.

⚠️ ABSOLUTELY NO! Touching hot springs is extremely dangerous and potentially fatal. Yellowstone's thermal features are among the most dangerous natural hazards in any national park.

The Dangers:

  • Scalding Temperatures: Many springs exceed 200°F (93°C) - hot enough to cause severe burns in seconds
  • Thin Crust: The ground around thermal features often has a thin, fragile crust that can break through
  • Toxic Gases: Hydrogen sulfide and other dangerous gases are often present
  • Acidic Water: Some springs are extremely acidic and can cause chemical burns
  • Unstable Ground: The thermal areas have unstable, scalding ground that can collapse

Tragic Statistics: Since Yellowstone's establishment, over 20 people have died from thermal feature accidents, and many more have suffered severe burns. Most accidents occur when people leave designated trails or boardwalks.

Safety Rules:

  • Always stay on designated boardwalks and trails
  • Keep children and pets close and under control
  • Never throw objects into thermal features
  • Don't test water temperature with any body part
  • Be extra cautious in areas without barriers
  • Follow all posted warning signs

Why the Rules Exist: The boardwalks and barriers aren't just suggestions - they're literally lifesavers. The park service has carefully studied these thermal areas and placed safety infrastructure where it's needed most.

Emergency Info: If someone falls into a thermal feature, call 911 immediately and contact the nearest ranger station. Do NOT attempt a rescue yourself as you may become a second victim.

Remember: You can enjoy Yellowstone's incredible thermal features safely by respecting the rules and maintaining a safe distance. The beauty is best appreciated from the designated viewing areas!
