Yellowstone National Park FAQ

Where is Yellowstone National Park located?

Yellowstone National Park is located primarily in Wyoming, but it also stretches into Montana and Idaho. The park features over 2 million acres of forests, lakes, and geothermal wonders.

Why is Yellowstone famous?

Yellowstone is famous for its geysers, hot springs, wildlife, and stunning landscapes. It was the first national park in the world, established in 1872 to preserve its natural beauty.

What is Old Faithful?

Old Faithful is a highly predictable geyser that erupts roughly every 60–110 minutes, shooting boiling water high into the air. It is one of the park's most iconic attractions.

When is the best time to visit Yellowstone?

The best time to visit Yellowstone is late spring or early fall when the weather is mild, wildlife is active, and crowds are smaller. Winter visits offer snow sports and fewer tourists.

Is it safe to touch hot springs?

No, touching hot springs is extremely dangerous. Some springs can reach temperatures over 200°F (93°C). Always stay on designated boardwalks and follow park safety guidelines.
